General Information

The Centre for Sustainability and Management Research (CSMR) publishes a range of peer-reviewed journals focused on sustainability and management research. All manuscripts submitted to CSMR journals must:

  • Be original work not previously published or under consideration elsewhere
  • Be relevant to the aims and scope of the specific journal
  • Follow ethical guidelines for research and publication
  • Adhere to the formatting requirements outlined below
  • Be written in clear, grammatically correct English

Authors are encouraged to review recent issues of the target journal to ensure their manuscript aligns with the journal's focus and style.

Manuscript Types

CSMR journals accept the following types of manuscripts:

Original Research Articles

Reports of original research, presenting new empirical findings, theoretical developments, or methodological innovations.

Length: 6,000-8,000 words (excluding references, tables, and figures)

Review Articles

Comprehensive, critical reviews of the literature on a specific topic, identifying research gaps and future directions.

Length: 7,000-10,000 words (excluding references, tables, and figures)

Case Studies

In-depth analyses of specific organizations, sustainability initiatives, or management practices.

Length: 4,000-6,000 words (excluding references, tables, and figures)

Perspective Articles

Scholarly opinion pieces or commentary on current issues, trends, or debates in sustainability and management.

Length: 3,000-5,000 words (excluding references, tables, and figures)

Research Notes

Brief reports on preliminary findings, novel methods, or emerging research areas.

Length: 2,000-3,000 words (excluding references, tables, and figures)

Formatting Guidelines

File Format

Submit manuscripts as Microsoft Word (.doc or .docx) files. PDFs are not accepted for initial submissions.

Page Setup

  • A4 page size
  • 2.5 cm (1 inch) margins on all sides
  • Line spacing: 1.5
  • Page numbers in the bottom right corner

Font

  • Main text: Times New Roman, 12 point
  • Headings: Times New Roman, 14 point (bold)
  • Subheadings: Times New Roman, 12 point (bold)

Tables and Figures

  • Place each table or figure on a separate page at the end of the manuscript
  • Indicate approximate placement in the text (e.g., [Insert Table 1 here])
  • Include clear, descriptive captions for all tables and figures
  • Ensure tables and figures are legible and of high quality
  • For graphs and charts, provide the underlying data in a separate file

Language

Manuscripts must be written in clear, grammatically correct English (American or British spelling is acceptable, but must be consistent throughout). Authors whose native language is not English are encouraged to have their manuscript reviewed by a native English speaker or to use professional editing services before submission.

Manuscript Structure

Manuscripts should generally include the following sections:

Title Page

Include the following information:

  • Manuscript title (concise and informative, max 20 words)
  • Author names and affiliations (institution, city, country)
  • Corresponding author's contact details (email, address, phone)
  • Word count (excluding references, tables, and figures)

Abstract

A structured abstract of 200-250 words, including:

  • Purpose
  • Design/methodology/approach
  • Findings
  • Research limitations/implications
  • Practical implications
  • Social implications
  • Originality/value

Keywords

4-6 keywords or phrases that reflect the content of the manuscript.

Main Text

The main text should typically include:

  1. Introduction: Background, research problem, objectives, and structure of the paper
  2. Literature Review: Critical analysis of relevant literature and theoretical framework
  3. Methodology: Research design, data collection, and analysis methods
  4. Results: Presentation of key findings without interpretation
  5. Discussion: Interpretation of findings, comparison with existing literature
  6. Conclusion: Summary of key findings, implications, limitations, and future research directions

Acknowledgments

Acknowledge any funding sources, institutions, or individuals who contributed to the research but do not qualify for authorship.

References

List of all sources cited in the text, following the appropriate citation style (see References & Citations section).

Appendices

Supplementary material that would interrupt the flow of the main text but is important for understanding the research.

References & Citations

Citation Style

CSMR journals use the APA (American Psychological Association) 7th Edition style for citations and references.

In-Text Citations

Examples:

  • Single author: (Smith, 2020)
  • Two authors: (Smith & Jones, 2020)
  • Three or more authors: (Smith et al., 2020)
  • Multiple citations: (Smith, 2020; Jones, 2019)
  • Direct quote: (Smith, 2020, p. 45)

Reference List

Examples:

Journal Article:
Smith, J., & Jones, K. (2020). Sustainable business models in emerging markets. Journal of Sustainable Management, 8(2), 112-135. https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxx

Book:
Brown, M. (2019). Sustainability management: Theory and practice. Publisher Name.

Book Chapter:
Johnson, P. (2021). Green supply chain management. In R. Williams (Ed.), Sustainable operations management (pp. 123-145). Publisher Name.

Conference Paper:
Lee, S., & Kim, H. (2022, June 15-17). Digital transformation for sustainability in manufacturing [Paper presentation]. International Conference on Sustainable Management, Singapore.

Website:
United Nations. (2023, January 5). Sustainable Development Goals. https://www.un.org/sustainabledevelopment/

Submission Process

1. Register and Log In

Create an account on the CSMR submission portal at submit.csmr.org. If you already have an account, log in using your credentials.

2. Select Journal

Choose the appropriate journal for your manuscript from the dropdown menu.

3. Complete Submission Form

Provide all required information, including:

  • Manuscript type
  • Title and abstract
  • Author information
  • Keywords
  • Suggested reviewers (optional)
  • Excluded reviewers (optional, with justification)

4. Upload Files

Upload the following files:

  • Main manuscript (without author information for blind review)
  • Title page (with all author information)
  • Figures (if any, in high-resolution format)
  • Tables (if any, in editable format)
  • Supplementary material (if any)
  • Cover letter (addressed to the Editor-in-Chief)

5. Review and Submit

Review all information and files, then click "Submit" to complete the process. You will receive an email confirmation of your submission.

Cover Letter

The cover letter should include:

  • Manuscript title
  • Brief description of the research and its significance
  • Confirmation that the work is original and not under consideration elsewhere
  • Explanation of how the manuscript fits the journal's scope
  • Any special considerations or requests

Peer Review Process

Double-Blind Review

CSMR journals employ a double-blind peer review process, in which the identities of both authors and reviewers are concealed. This helps ensure objective evaluation of manuscripts.

Review Stages

  1. Initial Screening: Editor-in-Chief or Associate Editor reviews the manuscript for scope, quality, and adherence to guidelines. Manuscripts that do not meet basic criteria may be rejected without peer review.
  2. Peer Review: Manuscripts that pass initial screening are sent to 2-3 expert reviewers who evaluate the manuscript based on originality, methodology, theoretical contribution, and practical implications.
  3. Decision: Based on reviewer recommendations, the Editor makes one of the following decisions:
    • Accept without revisions
    • Accept with minor revisions
    • Revise and resubmit (major revisions)
    • Reject with recommendation to submit elsewhere
    • Reject
  4. Revision: Authors address reviewer comments and resubmit the revised manuscript, along with a point-by-point response to each reviewer comment.
  5. Final Decision: Editor reviews the revised manuscript and responses, and makes a final decision.

Review Timeline

  • Initial screening: 1-2 weeks
  • Peer review: 4-6 weeks
  • Revision period: 2-4 weeks (depending on the extent of revisions)
  • Final decision after revision: 2-3 weeks

Total time from submission to first decision is typically 5-8 weeks. The entire process from submission to publication may take 3-6 months, depending on the revision requirements and journal publication schedule.

Publication Fees

Article Processing Charges (APCs)

CSMR operates on a mixed model of open access and subscription-based publishing. Publication fees vary by journal and access model:

Journal Type Fee Structure Amount
Full Open Access Journals Article Processing Charge (APC) ₹20,000 / $250
Hybrid Journals (OA Option) Article Processing Charge (APC) ₹30,000 / $375
Subscription-Based Journals No charge to authors Free

Fee Waivers and Discounts

CSMR is committed to ensuring that financial constraints do not prevent the publication of high-quality research. We offer:

  • Complete waivers for researchers from low-income countries (as defined by the World Bank)
  • Partial waivers (50%) for researchers from lower-middle-income countries
  • Discounts (20-30%) for early-career researchers, students, and authors from partner institutions

To apply for a waiver or discount, authors should contact waivers@csmr.org before submission, providing appropriate documentation.

Payment Process

APCs are payable only after a manuscript has been accepted for publication. Authors will receive an invoice with payment instructions. Payment can be made via bank transfer, credit card, or institutional payment systems.
