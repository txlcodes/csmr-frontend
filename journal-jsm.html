About the Journal

The Journal of Sustainable Management (JSM) was established in 2017 by the Centre for Sustainability and Management Research (CSMR) as a platform for advancing knowledge in sustainable management practices through rigorous, peer-reviewed research.

JSM publishes original research articles, review papers, case studies, and perspective pieces that explore the intersection of sustainability principles and management practices. The journal welcomes contributions from a wide range of disciplinary perspectives, including management, economics, environmental science, policy studies, and related fields.

Aims and Scope

JSM aims to advance understanding of sustainable management practices that integrate environmental, social, and economic considerations into business operations and strategy. The journal focuses on both theoretical developments and practical applications, with particular attention to:

  • Sustainable business models and strategies
  • Corporate sustainability and responsible management
  • Environmental management systems and policies
  • Sustainable supply chain management
  • Circular economy implementation
  • Sustainability reporting and performance measurement
  • Sustainability-oriented innovation
  • Corporate social responsibility and stakeholder engagement
  • Sustainable consumption and production
  • Policy frameworks for sustainable business

The journal is particularly interested in research that examines these topics in diverse geographic contexts, with special emphasis on emerging economies and developing markets.

Sustainable Business Models in Post-Pandemic India: Challenges and Opportunities for SMEs

Priya Sharma, Rahul Verma, Anjali Desai

This study examines how small and medium enterprises in India have adapted their business models to address sustainability challenges in the post-pandemic economy. Through a mixed-methods approach involving surveys of 150 SMEs and in-depth interviews with 25 business leaders, the research identifies key factors enabling sustainable transitions.

Corporate Engagement with SDGs: Evidence from Indian Public Sector Enterprises

Sameer Mathur, Leela Krishnan, Wei Liu

This paper analyzes the engagement of Indian public sector enterprises with the UN Sustainable Development Goals through content analysis of sustainability reports and in-depth interviews with sustainability managers. Results reveal patterns of SDG prioritization and implementation challenges in the public sector context.

Strategic Implementation of ESG Criteria in Investment Decisions: A Comparative Study of Asset Managers in Emerging Markets

Vikram Singh, Elena Petrova, Rajiv Mehta

This research investigates how asset managers in emerging markets integrate environmental, social, and governance (ESG) criteria into investment decision-making processes. Through comparative case studies of asset management firms in India, Brazil, and South Africa, the paper identifies institutional factors influencing ESG integration.

Green Human Resource Management Practices and Employee Engagement: Evidence from Manufacturing Sector

Arjun Kumar, Sarah Wilson, Deepa Patel

This study examines the relationship between green human resource management practices and employee engagement in sustainability initiatives. Using data from 250 employees across 15 manufacturing organizations, the research tests a model linking green HRM practices to environmental performance through the mediating effect of employee engagement.
