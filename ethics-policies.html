Publication Ethics and Academic Integrity

The Centre for Sustainability and Management Research (CSMR) is committed to maintaining the highest standards of publication ethics and academic integrity across all our journals. We adhere to the principles and guidelines established by the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE), the International Committee of Medical Journal Editors (ICMJE), and the World Association of Medical Editors (WAME).

All CSMR stakeholders—including authors, reviewers, editors, and staff—are expected to follow these ethical guidelines to ensure the quality and integrity of published research.

Author Responsibilities

Originality and Plagiarism

Authors must submit only original work that has not been published or under consideration elsewhere. All sources must be properly cited, and text, data, or ideas borrowed from others must be acknowledged. CSMR uses plagiarism detection software to screen all submissions.

Data Integrity and Fabrication

Authors must present their results clearly, honestly, and without fabrication, falsification, or inappropriate data manipulation. Research methods should be described clearly to allow for replication. When requested, authors should make their data available for verification.

Authorship Criteria

Authorship should be limited to those who have made a significant contribution to the conception, design, execution, or interpretation of the reported study. All those who have made significant contributions should be listed as co-authors. Others who have participated in certain substantive aspects of the research project should be acknowledged or listed as contributors.

The corresponding author should ensure that all appropriate co-authors are included on the paper, and that all co-authors have seen and approved the final version of the paper and have agreed to its submission for publication.

Multiple, Redundant, or Concurrent Publication

Authors should not publish manuscripts describing essentially the same research in more than one journal. Submitting the same manuscript to more than one journal concurrently constitutes unethical behavior and is unacceptable.

Disclosure and Conflicts of Interest

Authors must disclose any financial or other substantive conflicts of interest that might influence the results or interpretation of their manuscript. All sources of financial support for the project should be disclosed.

Fundamental Errors in Published Works

When an author discovers a significant error or inaccuracy in their own published work, it is the author's obligation to promptly notify the journal editor or publisher and cooperate to retract or correct the paper.

Reviewer Responsibilities

Contribution to Editorial Decisions

Peer review assists the editor in making editorial decisions and, through editorial communications with the author, may also assist the author in improving the paper.

Promptness

Any reviewer who feels unqualified to review the research reported in a manuscript or knows that its timely review will be impossible should notify the editor and decline the review process.

Confidentiality

Any manuscripts received for review must be treated as confidential documents. They must not be shown to or discussed with others except if authorized by the editor.

Standards of Objectivity

Reviews should be conducted objectively. Personal criticism of the author is inappropriate. Reviewers should express their views clearly with appropriate supporting arguments.

Acknowledgment of Sources

Reviewers should identify relevant published work that has not been cited by the authors. Any statement that is an observation, derivation, or argument previously reported should be accompanied by the relevant citation. A reviewer should also call to the editor's attention any substantial similarity or overlap between the manuscript under consideration and any other published paper of which they have personal knowledge.

Disclosure and Conflicts of Interest

Privileged information or ideas obtained through peer review must be kept confidential and not used for personal advantage. Reviewers should not consider manuscripts in which they have conflicts of interest resulting from competitive, collaborative, or other relationships with any of the authors, companies, or institutions connected to the papers.

Editor Responsibilities

Publication Decisions

The editor of a journal is responsible for deciding which of the articles submitted to the journal should be published. The editor may be guided by the policies of the journal's editorial board and constrained by such legal requirements as shall then be in force regarding libel, copyright infringement, and plagiarism.

Fair Play

Editors should evaluate manuscripts for their intellectual content without regard to race, gender, sexual orientation, religious belief, ethnic origin, citizenship, or political philosophy of the authors.

Confidentiality

The editor and any editorial staff must not disclose any information about a submitted manuscript to anyone other than the corresponding author, reviewers, potential reviewers, other editorial advisers, and the publisher, as appropriate.

Disclosure and Conflicts of Interest

Unpublished materials disclosed in a submitted manuscript must not be used in an editor's own research without the express written consent of the author. Privileged information or ideas obtained through peer review must be kept confidential and not used for personal advantage.

Research Ethics

Research Involving Human Participants

Research involving human participants must be conducted in accordance with the Declaration of Helsinki and must have been approved by an appropriate ethics committee. Authors must include a statement in their manuscript confirming that informed consent was obtained from all participants.

Research Involving Animals

Research involving animals must be conducted in accordance with international standards for animal welfare. Authors must include a statement in their manuscript confirming that the research was conducted with appropriate ethical approval and followed relevant guidelines.

Consent for Publication

For case reports or studies involving individual participants, authors must have obtained written informed consent for publication from the participants or their legal guardians.

Ethical Considerations in Field Research

Field research should respect local customs, cultural traditions, and legal requirements. Researchers should obtain appropriate permissions for field data collection and minimize their impact on the environment and communities.

Publication Integrity

Corrections and Retractions

When significant errors are discovered in published articles, CSMR will follow COPE guidelines for corrections, expressions of concern, or retractions. The type of notice depends on the nature and severity of the issue:

  • Correction: For minor errors that do not affect the findings or conclusions.
  • Expression of Concern: When there are suspected issues but insufficient evidence for a conclusion.
  • Retraction: For serious issues affecting the validity of findings or ethical concerns.

Post-Publication Discussions

CSMR encourages scholarly debate and discussion of published work. Authors of critiques must follow the same ethical guidelines as other authors, and subjects of critique will be given an opportunity to respond.

Data Sharing and Reproducibility

CSMR encourages authors to share data, code, and materials that support their research findings. Authors should provide a data availability statement indicating how and where data can be accessed, or why data cannot be shared.

Conflicts of Interest

Definition and Disclosure

A conflict of interest exists when professional judgment concerning a primary interest (such as the validity of research) may be influenced by a secondary interest (such as financial gain). All participants in the peer-review and publication process must disclose all relationships that could be viewed as potential conflicts of interest.

Types of Conflicts

Conflicts of interest may include, but are not limited to:

  • Financial interests (such as ownership, stock holdings, consultancies, honoraria)
  • Personal relationships
  • Academic competition
  • Intellectual beliefs
  • Political or religious convictions
  • Institutional affiliations

Declaration Process

Authors must disclose conflicts of interest in a dedicated section of their manuscript and in the online submission form. Editors and reviewers must disclose conflicts of interest that might affect their evaluation of a manuscript and recuse themselves when appropriate.

Copyright & Licensing

Copyright Policy

For subscription-based journals, authors typically transfer copyright to CSMR upon acceptance of their article. For open access journals, authors retain copyright but grant CSMR an exclusive license to publish the article.

Open Access Licensing

Open access articles are published under a Creative Commons license, typically CC BY (Attribution) or CC BY-NC-ND (Attribution-NonCommercial-NoDerivatives). These licenses allow others to reuse the work under specific conditions while ensuring proper attribution to the original authors.

Self-Archiving Policy

CSMR supports green open access, allowing authors to self-archive specific versions of their work in institutional or subject repositories according to the following terms:

  • Preprint: Authors may share preprints at any time.
  • Accepted Manuscript: May be shared after a 12-month embargo for subscription articles.
  • Published Version: May be shared immediately for open access articles with the appropriate CC license.

Handling Misconduct

Reporting Suspected Misconduct

CSMR takes allegations of misconduct seriously. Anyone who suspects misconduct in research or publication ethics should report their concerns to the relevant journal editor or to ethics@csmr.org. All allegations will be handled confidentially.

Investigation Process

CSMR follows COPE guidelines for handling allegations of misconduct:

  1. Initial assessment of the allegation
  2. Collection of evidence and investigation
  3. Communication with involved parties
  4. Decision-making based on the evidence
  5. Implementation of appropriate actions

Potential Outcomes

Depending on the nature and severity of the misconduct, CSMR may take one or more of the following actions:

  • Rejection of a manuscript
  • Publication of a correction
  • Publication of an expression of concern
  • Retraction of a published article
  • Notification to the author's institution or funding body
  • Ban on future submissions from authors found to have committed serious misconduct

Appeals and Disagreements

Authors who wish to appeal an editorial decision or who disagree with the handling of an ethical issue may contact the Editor-in-Chief or the Ethics Committee at appeals@csmr.org.

Our Commitment to Ethical Research

The Centre for Sustainability and Management Research is dedicated to promoting ethical research practices and maintaining the highest standards of academic integrity. We believe that ethical research is not just a requirement but a fundamental principle that underpins the advancement of knowledge.
